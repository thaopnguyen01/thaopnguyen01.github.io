About

A short biography, a career arc, and a few ways to get in touch.

Thao Nguyen

When I was a kid, my favorite show was Doraemon — a robotic cat from the 22nd century who pulls impossibly useful gadgets out of a fourth-dimensional pocket to help his clumsy friend Nobita through ordinary trouble.

The episodes I remember most weren't the ones where the gadget solved the problem. They were the ones where it worked too well, or in directions no one had thought to check. A device that lets you trade perspectives. A pill that makes everyone agreeable. A flashlight that shrinks anything it touches. The gadget was never the lesson; the consequences were.

I think about Doraemon a lot in my work.

I'm a program and innovation manager working where AI/ML systems meet public health, consumer safety, and the unglamorous logistics of getting careful work shipped. Most of what I do, on a good day, is sit between teams that are speaking past each other — model builders and safety policy, public health practitioners and platform engineers, executives and the frontline operators absorbing the actual cost of an early call. The discipline I've built up over the years is in keeping those rooms honest with each other long enough to make a decision someone will still defend in six months.

A few things I care about, in no particular order: taxonomies that survive contact with the work. Evaluation that doesn't flatter the model. Pilot designs that allow a clean "no." Tooling that lives where the work lives, not where the budget thinks it should. And the patient, deeply unsexy craft of cross-functional program management — which I increasingly believe is one of the highest-leverage roles in AI right now.

Outside of work, I cook a lot, travel slowly when I can, and keep a long list of books I'm always one weekend away from finishing.
